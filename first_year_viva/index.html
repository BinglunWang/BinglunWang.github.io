First Year VIVA Video Results

PD Controlled Swimming

This illustrates the motion of an articulated character swimming in a fluid, viewed from the side and top side. The character's joint movements are controlled by joint torques from a PD controller output. The forward and rotational motion are produced entirely by the simulated hydrodynamic forces, demonstrating a physically-based interaction between the articulated body and its environment.

Stable Simulator Demonstration

System Stability Test

During initial integration, the system was unstable. After a systematic hyperparameter search, we achieved a stable system with a 50x speedup. This video validates the final system's robustness by applying various external joint torque and a forward forces on root to the character.

PPO control in zero-gravity environment

PPO Training in Zero Gravity

As a simplification, we trained a policy to "swim" in air with zero gravity. While successful for learning basic patterns, it did not capture the complexities of fluid interaction and could not adapt to a fluid environment.

Ablation: Single DoF PPO Control

We reduced task complexity by training a policy for a single arm DoF, with other 5 DoFs control by a PD controller with data input. Within half a day training, this 1-DoF policy learned a swimming strategy. These videos show the resulting motion.

Zero-Shot Transfer from local phases input

Our recent approach version controls motion with frequency domain input. After training the policy in a zero-gravity environment, we performed a zero-shot transfer into the full fluid simulation. The resulting motion, achieved without any direct training in the fluid, successfully exhibited a coherent swimming pattern.
